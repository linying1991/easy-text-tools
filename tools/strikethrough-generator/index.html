Frequently Asked Questions

What is strikethrough text?

Strikethrough text has a line drawn through it to indicate deletion, correction, or to show emphasis in a different way. It's commonly used to indicate crossed-out content or for stylistic purposes.

What types of strikethrough styles are available?

Our tool offers three styles: Single line (s̶t̶r̶i̶k̶e̶t̶h̶r̶o̶u̶g̶h̶), Double line (s̳t̳r̳i̳k̳e̳t̳h̳r̳o̳u̳g̳h̳), and Slash (s̷t̷r̷i̷k̷e̷t̷h̷r̷o̷u̷g̷h̷). Each creates a different visual effect.

How does strikethrough text work?

The tool uses Unicode combining characters that overlay the strikethrough line on each character. These are special Unicode characters (U+0336 for single, U+0333 for double, U+0337 for slash) that combine with base characters.

Will strikethrough text work everywhere?

Strikethrough text works in most modern applications that support Unicode combining characters, including social media, messaging apps, and most document editors. However, support may vary depending on the system and font used.
